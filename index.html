Johan Cruyff

The Flying Dutchman

Here's a timeline Cruyff's life:

  • 1947 - Born on the 25th of April in the Burgerziekenhuis hospital in Amsterdam.
  • 1957 - Joins the Ajax youth system on his tenth birthday.
  • 1959 - Loses his father to a heart attack. His father's death has a major impact on his mentality.
  • 1964 - Makes his first team debut on the 15th of November in the Eredivisie against GVAV. He scores the only goal for Ajax in a 3-1 defeat.
  • 1966 - Wins his first league title with Ajax. He scores 25 goals in 23 games during the season.
  • 1971 - Wins his first European Cup as a player. Ajax beat greek champions Panathinaikos 2-0 in the final in London. He was named European Footballer of the year for his performances.
  • 1973 - Signs for spanish side FC Barcelona for a world record transfer fee.
  • 1978 - Briefly retires, but after a series of poor investments drain his finances, resumes his playing career in the United States.
  • 1980 - Returns to Ajax, helping the club win two more league titles.
  • 1984 - Retires as a player at Feyenoord, having helped them win a first Eredivisie title in a decade.
  • 1988 - Returns to Barcelona, now a first team coach, and would go on to assemble one of the greatest teams in football history.
  • 1996 - Leaves Barcelona, becoming their most successful and longest serving manager at the time.
  • 2016 - On 24 March after a long cancer battle, passes away at the age of 68 surrounded by his family.

"He has enriched and personified our football. He was an icon in the Netherlands. Johan Cruijff belonged to all of us."

-- King Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands pays tribute following Cruyff's death.
